Quality Improvement

 

The Quality Improvement standards for AnciCare services focus on the following areas:   

  • timeliness of call answering;

  • nature of the information exchange;

  • quality of the information collected;

  • timeliness of contact with the injured worker;

  • timeliness of scheduling the claimant’s medical imaging study;

  • timeliness of notification to claims adjusters, case managers, and referring physicians of the scheduled study;

  • timeliness of receipt of the medical imaging report from the contracted medical imaging center;

  • timeliness of forwarding the medical imaging report to the appropriate parties; and

  •  Quality of medical imaging studies conducted by AnciCare providers.  

In addition, AnciCare evaluates the quality of services provided to claimants by our contracted providers through use of “Provider Flags”.  AnciCare utilizes Provider Flags to identify potential inappropriate behavior of providers.  The following may generate provider Flags :   

  • Intake Coordinators - based upon concerns voiced by claims adjusters or case managers;

  • Customer Service Representatives - based upon interaction with contracted providers, case managers and claims adjusters; or

  • Vice President of Operations - based upon interaction with our customers and with referring physicians.  

Aberrant practices which may be identified via a Provider Flag include but are not limited to:   

  • unduly long waits for scheduled medical imaging testing; 

  • repeated incidents of poor film quality;

  • complaints from a claimant regarding treatment rendered.  

Once a provider flag is generated, it is referred to the Quality Improvement Committee for analysis and trending.  Quality Improvement information is discussed quarterly with the Quality Improvement Committee, which consists of an MD Board Certified Radiologist and senior members of operations.  The Quality Improvement Committee meets on a regular basis to review provider flags and internal quality improvement reports.  In the event the Quality Improvement Committee feels a provider has displayed poor care, the supporting information is forwarded to the President of the company to review with legal counsel.  A variety of disciplinary actions may be applied including:   

  • one-on-one discussion with the provider;

  • focused retrospective review of cases by the clinical staff;

  • contract termination for a network provider; and/or

  • notification of appropriate licensing bodies, in the most serious cases.

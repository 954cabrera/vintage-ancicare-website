Case Coordination

 

When a request for medical imaging examination is received, AnciCare client service representative will ask the claims professional or case manager the following questions:   

  • Adjuster Name

  • Insurance Company

  • Ajuster Phone Number

  • Adjuster Fax Number

  • Case Manager Name

  • Case Manager Company

  • Case Manager Phone Number

  • Case Manager Fax Number

  • Referring Physician

  • Claimant Name

  • Claimant Address

  • Claimant Phone - Home and Work

  • Employer

  • Date of Birth

  • Social Security Number

  • Type of Claim (Workers’ Compensation, Auto, Group)

  • Injury Date

  • Claim Number

  • Diagnosis

  • Procedure Ordered (MRI, CT or Bone Scan, etc.)

Channeling injured employees to network providers is based on protocols established by AnciCare related to the physical and medical condition of the claimant.  Immediately after receiving the request for examination, AnciCare service representatives will call the claimant and ask pertinent questions about their medical condition, their height and weight, whether or not they have had any surgical procedures and if metal implants (i.e., plate, pins, wires) were placed in their body.  We also ask if they have done any welding (for potential metal fragments in the eyes) and if they have ever suffered symptoms of claustrophobia.   

If we do not reach the injured worker within two days of request of the medical imaging study, a letter will be sent to the last known address requesting that the claimant call AnciCare to schedule their medical imaging study.  AnciCare will call each person associated with the file to attempt to verify address, that is, the claims adjuster, case manager, and referring physician.  AnciCare will inform you if we are unable to contact the claimant.   

In the event the claimant needs transportation, we will make arrangements for them.  Many of our MRI centers provide free transportation, which we will arrange.  If free transportation is not available, we will contact you to get authorization to arrange transportation through a reputable company.   

Once we have determined the medical and physical condition of the claimant, we will schedule the medical imaging study at the appropriate center that is closest to the claimant’s home or job.  Our triage assures that the claimant is scheduled in the center that has the most appropriate equipment to accommodate any special needs.  

After we have secured an appointment with an AnciCare center, we will call the claimant again to confirm the appointment.  At this point, we will contact the claims adjuster, case manager and physician, via faxed confirmation form, to inform that the appointment has been scheduled.  

Finally, AnciCare will send the medical imaging report to the claims adjuster and case manager via facsimile within 48 hours of the completed procedure.

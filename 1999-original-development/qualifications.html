QUALIFICATIONS


We believe that employees with work related injuries should receive the most appropriate treatment in the most appropriate setting.  AnciCare has found that thorough and extensive credentialing of provider applications allows us to build networks comprised of quality providers where additional savings to our clients will be achieved.  In addition, AnciCare strives to contract with free-standing MRI centers that are owned by corporations or radiologists.

AnciCare values the quality of high field MRI magnets, which are cylinder bores. However, due to the nature of workers’ compensation, where the majority of MRIs ordered are of the spine, it is often required to have a variety of MRI equipment accessible, in particularly, open MRI units. 

Open MRI units are convenient to those patients that are either extremely claustrophobic and/or obese.  AnciCare has strategically selected MRI centers that provide outstanding MRI and CT services with state-of-the-art equipment, such as Siemens, Hitachi, and GE.  These companies are considered manufacturing leaders in the MRI industry.  

Once the application process has been completed and a provider has met credentialing criteria, contracts are negotiated between AnciCare and the provider.  Negotiations focus on the ability of the provider to deliver the specific services being sought by AnciCare and on the provider’s agreement to work with our case coordinators to meet the goals of our medical imaging program.   

AnciCare will customize the PPO to meet the needs of your clients who may be located in areas that are not heavily populated with contracted providers.  AnciCare will request opinions and preferences from those claims professionals and case managers who have a working knowledge of providers who have served your company well for particular clients.  Every effort will be made to recruit those providers requested at our preferred rates.

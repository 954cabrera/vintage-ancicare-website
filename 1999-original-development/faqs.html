How does Ancicare schedule a medical imaging procedure?

Either the adjuster or case manager, or after authorization, at the direction of the adjuster or case manager, the referring physician's office calls 1-800-414-4MRI or faxes the referral to (954) 441-6350 to schedule an appointment.  A personalized Fax Scheduling form will be prepared for you.

How far does the claimant have to drive for their appointment? 

We schedule the appointment at the facility nearest to the patient, usually within 15 minutes from their home or workplace.

Does AnciCare contact the physician, adjuster, case manager and the claimant?

Yes.  After we have confirmed the appointment with the patient, AnciCare will confirm the appointment via a fax confirmation and inform the referring physician's office, adjuster and/or case manager to let them know when and where the patient is scheduled.

How do you handle non-compliant patients?

The day following the appointment, we call the facility to confirm that the patient completed the exam.  If not, we find out why and reschedule when appropriate.  We will then notify the referring physician's office, adjuster and/or case manager regarding the status of the appointment.

How fast is the report turnaround to the physician?

Once the report has been prepared, the medical imaging facility faxes the medical report to the referring physician and AnciCare.  AnciCare then faxes the medical report to the adjuster and/or case manager within 24-48 hours after the procedure has been completed.

Does AnciCare guarantee the quality of their medical imaging services?

Because our facilities are fully credentialed with standards of excellence, we guarantee the quality of each medical imaging procedure.   Our complaint-free mission is the best in the industry.

Does AnciCare charge for no-shows?

There is never a charge for a procedure not completed.

 

What is Magnetic Resonance Imaging?

Magnetic Resonance Imaging (MRI) is a diagnostic imaging technology that uses a strong magnet and radio frequency waves to produce pictures or "images" of your internal organs and structures. Because MRI allows your doctor to see inside your body from any angle with great clarity, it is giving doctors a wealth of information more quickly and in many cases, more economically than past tests and exploratory surgeries.

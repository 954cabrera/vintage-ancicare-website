AnciCare Network - FAQ 
 

FREQUENTLY ASKED QUESTIONS

  • Are you a scheduling service broker?

  • Can you guarantee volume to our facility?

  • How many covered lives are available within my service area?

  • Will your Network do an exclusive agreement with my facility?

  • Do you contract with mobile units?

  • How do I receive referrals from your network?

  • How can I maximize referral volume from your network?

  • How long does it take to become a participating provider?

  • How long is the credentialing process?

  • Do you require that our facility be ACR and/or JCAHO accredited for participation?

 

 

Are you a scheduling service broker? 

No. AnciCare is a fee for service preferred provider network for medical imaging services.  All participating  providers are directly contracted with AnciCare and are independent free standing facilities.

Can you guarantee volume to our facility? 

No. We will make every effort to utilize your facility.  It is a corporate policy not to saturate a particular community with too many contracted providers.  We will only contract with additional providers if we have need for a particular radiological service, and/or a certain type of machine. 

How many covered lives are available within my service area? 

This is a hard question to answer because we do not directly contract with the employer groups in your services area.  Our arrangements are with the workers’ compensation insurance carriers, case management companies and third party administrators.  We do not have access to the listing of their clients in your particular area.  The bulk of our referrals are workers’ compensation referrals, therefore, the number of covered lives would not apply.  A referral would only occur when an employee is injured and the referring physician orders medical imaging services. 

Will your Network do an exclusive agreement with my facility? 

No. However, we will only contract with another facility, if you can not offer a particular service, or if we can not get our patient scheduled at your facility within 2-3 business days. 

Do you contract with mobile units? 

In general we do not, however, we will make exceptions.  It depends on availability of medical imaging services in the community.  If it is a rural area, and the area hospital does not offer MRI services we will contract with the mobile unit as needed. 

How do I receive referrals from your network? 

Our client service representatives will call your facility directly to schedule the patient for their medical imaging appointment.  All referrals are scheduled by AnciCare.

How can I maximize referral volume from your network? 

If your contract pricing is competitive, and our patients are scheduled within 2-3 business days, and we receive your medical reports 48 hours after the patient was seen. 

How long does it take to become a participating provider? 

To process an agreement for participation will take 5 business days, provided the agreement is received signed, and the Diagnostic Facility Information page is completed with all of the required information.  This allows us to properly market your facility to our clients and schedule the patients appropriately. 

How long is the credentialing process?  

If the credentialing application for the facility and for the radiologist(s) is completed thoroughly, and returned with the required documentation, it will take up to 60 days.  However, your facility will be considered a provisional provider until we complete the credentialing review which takes 180 days. 

Do you require that our facility be ACR and/or JCAHO accredited for participation? 

Yes.  However, if your facility has applied for accreditation, we will accept your participation provided you will complete it by January 31, 2000.
